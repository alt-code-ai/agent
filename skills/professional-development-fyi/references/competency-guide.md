# FYI Competency Guide

Detailed development guidance for all 67 competencies from *FYI For Your Improvement* by Lombardo & Eichinger. Each entry provides: definition, behavioural indicators at three levels (unskilled, skilled, overused), common causes of being unskilled, development tips, and overuse remediation.

For competencies not detailed below, apply the same pattern: identify behaviours, diagnose root causes, and prescribe 70-20-10 development actions.

## 1. Action Oriented

**Definition:** Enjoys working hard. Is action-oriented and full of energy for the things seen as challenging. Not fearful of acting with minimum planning. Seizes more opportunities than others.

**Unskilled:** Slow to act. Overly cautious. Waits for all data before moving. Misses opportunities. May over-plan and under-execute. May be a perfectionist who can't ship.

**Skilled:** Seizes opportunities. Energetic and willing to try new things. Moves quickly from analysis to action. Comfortable with calculated risk.

**Overused:** Impatient. Acts before thinking. Doesn't plan enough. May run over people. Creates chaos by starting too many things. Doesn't finish what they start.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Volunteer for a high-urgency project with a tight deadline. Take on a task outside your comfort zone where you can't over-prepare.
- *Relationships:* Shadow someone known for bias toward action. Ask them how they decide when they have enough information to act.
- *Education:* Read *The Lean Startup* (Ries) — build-measure-learn cycles as a discipline of action.
- *Overuse remedy:* Before acting, write down the top 3 risks. Ask one person for input. Build in a 24-hour pause for high-stakes decisions.

## 2. Dealing with Ambiguity

**Definition:** Can effectively cope with change. Can shift gears comfortably. Can decide and act without having the total picture. Isn't upset when things are up in the air. Doesn't have to finish things before moving on. Can comfortably handle risk and uncertainty.

**Unskilled:** Needs clear direction and certainty. Gets anxious when things are undefined. Freezes or over-analyses in ambiguous situations. May demand premature closure.

**Skilled:** Comfortable in fluid, changing situations. Makes decisions with incomplete information. Adapts quickly. Manages their own anxiety about uncertainty.

**Overused:** May appear unfocused or chaotic. May create unnecessary ambiguity for others. May avoid committing to a direction. Can seem indecisive while being comfortable with the ambiguity.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Take on a project in an area you know nothing about. Lead a cross-functional initiative where goals are evolving. Work in a startup or innovation team.
- *Relationships:* Find a mentor who thrives in ambiguity. Observe how they make decisions without full data.
- *Education:* Study decision-making under uncertainty (e.g., *Thinking, Fast and Slow* by Kahneman).
- *Overuse remedy:* Provide more structure for your team even if you don't need it yourself. Communicate clear direction even when the path is uncertain. Make provisional decisions explicitly.

## 3. Approachability

**Definition:** Is easy to approach and talk to. Spends the extra effort to put others at ease. Can be warm, pleasant, and gracious. Is sensitive to and patient with the interpersonal anxieties of others. Builds rapport. Is a good listener. Is an early knower of what's going on.

**Unskilled:** May appear cold, distant, or intimidating. Hard to read. People don't share information voluntarily. May be overly formal. Gets information late.

**Skilled:** People seek them out. Makes others comfortable. Builds informal networks. Hears things early. Creates psychological safety.

**Overused:** May waste too much time on socialising. May avoid tough conversations to maintain likability. May be seen as a pushover.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Walk the floor / visit other teams regularly. Have informal 1:1s with no agenda. Attend events outside your normal circle.
- *Relationships:* Ask trusted colleagues: "Am I easy to approach? What would make me easier to approach?"
- *Overuse remedy:* Balance warmth with directness. Practice delivering hard messages with care.

## 4. Boss Relationships

**Definition:** Responds and relates well to bosses. Would work harder for a good boss. Is open to learning from bosses. Likes to learn from those who have more experience. Easy to challenge and develop. Is comfortably coachable.

**Unskilled:** Fights with bosses. Resists authority. Doesn't manage up effectively. May be seen as difficult to coach. Doesn't adapt to boss's style.

**Skilled:** Builds strong working relationships with supervisors. Adapts to their boss's communication and work style. Proactively keeps boss informed. Is coachable.

**Overused:** Too dependent on boss's approval. Won't act without direction. May appear sycophantic. Career may depend too much on one person.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Study your boss's priorities, communication preferences, and decision-making style. Proactively provide updates in the format they prefer.
- *Relationships:* Ask your boss directly: "What can I do to make your job easier?"
- *Overuse remedy:* Make decisions independently when appropriate. Build relationships with multiple senior leaders, not just your direct boss.

## 5. Business Acumen

**Definition:** Knows how businesses work. Knowledgeable in current and possible future policies, practices, trends, and information affecting the business and organisation. Knows the competition. Is aware of how strategies and tactics work in the marketplace.

**Unskilled:** Narrowly focused on own function. Doesn't understand the P&L. Can't explain how the company makes money. Lacks market awareness.

**Skilled:** Understands the full value chain. Can read a P&L. Follows industry trends and competitors. Makes decisions with business impact in mind.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Read your company's annual report and 10-K. Follow industry analyst reports. Attend customer meetings. Spend time with finance and operations.
- *Education:* Take a business fundamentals course. Read *The Personal MBA* (Kaufman) or *Good Strategy / Bad Strategy* (Rumelt).

## 11. Composure

**Definition:** Is cool under pressure. Does not become defensive or irritated when times are tough. Is considered mature. Can be counted on to hold things together during tough times. Can handle stress. Is not knocked off balance by the unexpected.

**Unskilled:** Gets visibly flustered. Snaps at people under pressure. Makes poor decisions when stressed. May become defensive or emotional.

**Skilled:** Stays calm and clear-headed in crisis. Doesn't take things personally. Manages their own emotional reactions. Others look to them for stability.

**Overused:** May appear cold, detached, or uncaring. May suppress emotions unhealthily. People may feel they don't care. May not show enough urgency.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Volunteer for high-pressure, high-visibility situations. Practice staying silent for 10 seconds before responding to provocative comments.
- *Relationships:* Find someone known for grace under pressure. Ask how they manage their internal state.
- *Overuse remedy:* Show appropriate emotion. Let people see that you care. Express empathy verbally, even when you feel calm internally.

## 12. Conflict Management

**Definition:** Steps up to conflicts, seeing them as opportunities. Reads situations quickly. Good at focused listening. Can hammer out tough agreements and settle disputes equitably. Can find common ground and get cooperation with minimum noise.

**Unskilled:** Avoids conflict. Lets issues fester. Gives in too easily. May become aggressive rather than assertive. Takes sides rather than mediating.

**Skilled:** Addresses conflict directly and early. Finds win-win solutions. Listens to all sides. Separates people from problems. De-escalates tension.

**Overused:** May create conflict unnecessarily. May enjoy the fight too much. May be seen as abrasive or confrontational.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Mediate a dispute between two colleagues. Lead a difficult negotiation. Address a performance issue you've been avoiding.
- *Education:* Read *Crucial Conversations* (Patterson et al.) or *Getting to Yes* (Fisher & Ury).
- *Overuse remedy:* Ask: "Does this conflict need me to engage, or will it resolve naturally?" Choose your battles.

## 14. Creativity

**Definition:** Comes up with a lot of new and unique ideas. Easily makes connections among previously unrelated notions. Tends to be seen as original and value-added in brainstorming settings.

**Unskilled:** Relies on proven approaches. Doesn't generate new ideas. May dismiss others' creative ideas. Prefers the safe path.

**Skilled:** Generates novel approaches. Makes unexpected connections. Creates an environment where others feel safe to experiment. Thinks beyond the obvious.

**Overused:** Impractical. Too many ideas, not enough execution. May ignore proven approaches. Can frustrate implementers.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Brainstorm 10 ideas for every problem (force beyond the obvious 3). Visit a different industry and look for transferable ideas. Work with a creative team on a non-work project.
- *Education:* Read *A Whack on the Side of the Head* (von Oech). Practice lateral thinking techniques.
- *Overuse remedy:* For every creative idea, write down the 3 steps to implement it. Partner with an executor.

## 15. Customer Focus

**Definition:** Is dedicated to meeting the expectations and requirements of internal or external customers. Gets first-hand customer information and uses it for improvements in products and services. Acts with customers in mind. Establishes and maintains effective relationships with customers and gains their trust and respect.

**Unskilled:** Makes decisions without considering customer impact. Doesn't seek customer feedback. Focuses on internal metrics over customer outcomes.

**Skilled:** Listens to customers. Anticipates needs. Responds quickly. Builds long-term relationships. Uses customer data to drive improvements.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Spend a day in customer support. Conduct 5 customer interviews this quarter. Attend a customer's site visit.
- *Relationships:* Find the person in your org who is most customer-obsessed. Shadow them.

## 17. Decision Quality

**Definition:** Makes good decisions based upon a mixture of analysis, wisdom, experience, and judgment. Most of the solutions and suggestions turn out to be correct and accurate when judged over time. Sought out by others for advice and solutions.

**Unskilled:** Decisions are often wrong or suboptimal. May over-analyse or act too hastily. Doesn't consider enough perspectives. Avoids making decisions.

**Skilled:** Makes sound decisions by combining data, experience, and intuition. Considers multiple perspectives. Balances speed and thoroughness.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* After each major decision, write a brief post-mortem: what did you know, what did you decide, what happened? Study your decision patterns.
- *Education:* Read *Decisive* (Heath & Heath) on common decision traps and countermeasures.

## 18. Delegation

**Definition:** Clearly and comfortably delegates both routine and important tasks and decisions. Broadly shares both responsibility and accountability. Tends to trust people to perform. Lets direct reports finish their own work.

**Unskilled:** Hoards work. Micromanages. Doesn't trust others to deliver. Becomes a bottleneck. Doesn't develop others by giving them real responsibility.

**Skilled:** Matches tasks to people's skills and development needs. Provides clear expectations and authority. Follows up without micromanaging. Celebrates others' success.

**Overused:** May abdicate rather than delegate. Doesn't stay close enough to important work. May not provide enough support or context.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Identify 3 tasks only you do that someone else could learn. Delegate them this month with clear outcomes and a check-in schedule.
- *Relationships:* Ask your direct reports: "What would you like me to delegate more of?"
- *Overuse remedy:* Stay connected through regular check-ins. Be available for questions. Don't disappear after delegating.

## 19. Developing Direct Reports & Others

**Definition:** Provides challenging and stretching tasks and assignments. Holds frequent development discussions. Is aware of each person's career goals. Constructs compelling development plans and executes them. Pushes people to accept developmental moves. Will take on those who need help and further development. Is a people builder.

**Unskilled:** Doesn't invest in others' growth. Doesn't know what their people aspire to. Gives only easy assignments. Avoids development conversations.

**Skilled:** Knows each person's aspirations and strengths. Creates stretch assignments. Gives direct, useful feedback. Advocates for others' advancement.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Have a development conversation with each direct report this week. Identify one stretch assignment per person this quarter.
- *Education:* Read *The Coaching Habit* (Stanier) — learn to ask instead of tell.

## 53. Drive for Results

**Definition:** Can be counted on to exceed goals successfully. Is constantly and consistently one of the top performers. Very bottom-line oriented. Steadfastly pushes self and others for results.

**Unskilled:** Misses targets. Doesn't set ambitious goals. Gives up when things get difficult. May be more interested in process than outcomes.

**Skilled:** Consistently delivers. Sets stretch goals. Pushes through obstacles. Holds self and others accountable.

**Overused:** Sacrifices people and relationships for results. Burns out self and team. Cuts corners ethically. Doesn't celebrate or pause.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Set one goal that requires collaboration, not just individual effort.
- *Overuse remedy:* Track not only *what* you achieved but *how* — did you leave relationships intact? Ask your team: "What's the cost of how we're working?"

## 29. Integrity and Trust

**Definition:** Is widely trusted. Is seen as a direct, truthful individual. Can present the unvarnished truth in an appropriate and helpful manner. Keeps confidences. Admits mistakes. Doesn't misrepresent themselves for personal gain.

**Unskilled:** Says one thing, does another. Breaks confidences. Shades the truth. Takes credit for others' work. Makes promises they don't keep.

**Skilled:** Walk matches talk. People confide in them. Admits mistakes openly. Gives credit where due. Keeps commitments.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* When you make a mistake, acknowledge it publicly within 24 hours. Follow through on every commitment, no matter how small.
- *Education:* Read *The Speed of Trust* (Covey) on the economics of trust.

---

## 31. Interpersonal Savvy

**Definition:** Relates well to all kinds of people, up, down, and sideways, inside and outside the organisation. Builds appropriate rapport. Builds constructive and effective relationships. Uses diplomacy and tact. Can defuse even high-tension situations comfortably.

**Unskilled:** Awkward in social situations. May be seen as blunt or abrasive. Doesn't read social cues. Has difficulty with people who are different.

**Skilled:** Builds rapport quickly. Adjusts communication style to the audience. Navigates difficult conversations gracefully. Comfortable across cultures and levels.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Have a meaningful conversation with someone very different from you this week. Practice adjusting your communication style to different audiences.
- *Relationships:* Ask someone you find difficult to work with for coffee. Seek to understand their perspective.

---

## 32. Learning on the Fly

**Definition:** Learns quickly when facing new problems. A relentless and versatile learner. Open to change. Analyzes both successes and failures for clues to improvement. Experiments and will try anything to find solutions. Enjoys the challenge of unfamiliar tasks.

**Unskilled:** Sticks to what they know. Slow to adapt. Repeats approaches even when they don't work. Uncomfortable with unfamiliarity.

**Skilled:** Picks up new skills quickly. Learns from mistakes. Seeks novel experiences. Extracts lessons from every situation.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Take on a project in an area completely new to you. After every significant experience, write down 3 things you learned.
- *Education:* Study learning-to-learn techniques. Read *Mindset* (Dweck) on growth vs. fixed mindset.

---

## 33. Listening

**Definition:** Practices attentive and active listening. Has the patience to hear people out. Can accurately restate the opinions of others even when they disagree.

**Unskilled:** Interrupts. Finishes people's sentences. Thinks about their response while the other person is still talking. Misses non-verbal cues. People feel unheard.

**Skilled:** Gives full attention. Paraphrases to confirm understanding. Asks follow-up questions. Comfortable with silence. People feel heard and valued.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* In your next 5 conversations, practice not speaking for the first 30 seconds after the other person finishes. Ask: "What else?" at least once.
- *Overuse remedy:* Listening without acting can frustrate people. After listening, summarise and commit to next steps.

## 34. Managerial Courage

**Definition:** Doesn't hold back anything that needs to be said. Provides current, direct, complete, and "actionable" positive and corrective feedback to others. Lets people know where they stand. Faces up to people problems on any person or situation quickly and directly. Is not afraid to take negative action when necessary.

**Unskilled:** Avoids difficult conversations. Sugarcoats feedback. Tolerates poor performance. Hopes problems will resolve themselves.

**Skilled:** Has tough conversations promptly and respectfully. Gives clear, specific feedback. Addresses performance issues directly. Says what others won't.

**Overused:** May be seen as harsh or insensitive. May not pick up on the emotional impact of directness. Can create fear rather than trust.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Identify the most difficult conversation you've been avoiding. Have it this week. Prepare your opening line and practice it.
- *Education:* Read *Radical Candor* (Scott) — care personally, challenge directly.
- *Overuse remedy:* Before delivering hard truths, explicitly show that you care about the person. Ask for their perspective first.

## 35. Managing and Measuring Work

**Definition:** Clearly assigns responsibility for tasks and decisions. Sets clear objectives and measures. Monitors process, progress, and results. Designs feedback loops into work.

**Unskilled:** Vague about expectations. Doesn't track progress. People don't know what success looks like. Surprises at review time.

**Skilled:** Sets SMART goals. Establishes clear milestones. Provides regular feedback. Creates dashboards and visibility into progress.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* For every project, define "done" in writing before starting. Create a simple weekly scorecard for your team's top 3 priorities.

---

## 36. Motivating Others

**Definition:** Creates a climate in which people want to do their best. Can motivate many kinds of direct reports and team or project members. Can assess what each person's hot buttons are and use them to get the best out of them. Pushes tasks and decisions down. Empowers others. Invites input. Shares ownership and visibility. Makes each individual feel their work is important.

**Unskilled:** Demotivates through micromanagement, blame, or indifference. Doesn't recognise good work. One-size-fits-all approach.

**Skilled:** Tailors motivation to each individual. Celebrates wins publicly. Gives meaningful autonomy. Connects work to purpose.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Ask each direct report: "What motivates you most at work?" Act on the answers.
- *Education:* Read *Drive* (Pink) — autonomy, mastery, and purpose as intrinsic motivators.

---

## 50. Priority Setting

**Definition:** Spends time and the time of others on what's important. Quickly zeros in on the critical few and puts the trivial many aside. Can quickly sense what will help or hinder accomplishing a goal. Eliminates roadblocks. Creates focus.

**Unskilled:** Tries to do everything. Can't distinguish urgent from important. Overcommits. Allows others to set their priorities. Busy but not productive.

**Skilled:** Ruthlessly focuses on the vital few. Says no to the rest. Creates clarity for their team. Eliminates low-value work.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* List everything on your plate. Force-rank by impact. Eliminate or delegate the bottom third.
- *Education:* Read *Essentialism* (McKeown) — the disciplined pursuit of less.

---

## 51. Problem Solving

**Definition:** Uses rigorous logic and methods to solve difficult problems with effective solutions. Can see hidden problems. Is excellent at honest analysis. Looks beyond the obvious and doesn't stop at the first answers.

**Unskilled:** Jumps to solutions. Doesn't define the problem clearly. Addresses symptoms, not root causes. Uses the same approach regardless of the problem type.

**Skilled:** Defines problems precisely. Considers multiple root causes. Generates and evaluates alternative solutions. Tests before committing.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Use the "5 Whys" on the next problem you encounter. Write down 3 possible solutions before choosing one.

---

## 55. Self-Knowledge

**Definition:** Knows personal strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and limits. Seeks feedback. Gains insights from mistakes. Is open to criticism. Is not defensive. Is receptive to talking about shortcomings.

**Unskilled:** Blind spots. Overestimates strengths. Defensive when receiving feedback. Doesn't seek input on performance. Surprised by negative feedback.

**Skilled:** Accurately assesses own capabilities. Actively seeks feedback. Reflects on performance. Adjusts behaviour based on self-awareness.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Ask 5 trusted colleagues for anonymous feedback on your top strength and top development area. Keep a reflection journal.
- *Education:* Take a validated assessment (360, MBTI, StrengthsFinder, Hogan).

---

## 58. Strategic Agility

**Definition:** Sees ahead clearly. Can anticipate future consequences and trends accurately. Has broad knowledge and perspective. Is future-oriented. Can articulately paint credible pictures and visions of possibilities and likelihoods. Can create competitive and breakthrough strategies and plans.

**Unskilled:** Tactical, not strategic. Can't see past current quarter. Doesn't connect industry trends to their business. Reactive rather than proactive.

**Skilled:** Thinks in 3–5 year horizons. Connects dots across industries and functions. Creates strategies that anticipate market shifts. Communicates a clear, compelling vision.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Read your industry's top 3 analyst reports. Present a "future of our business" perspective to your team. Attend a conference outside your field.
- *Education:* Read *Good Strategy / Bad Strategy* (Rumelt).

---

## 60. Building Effective Teams

**Definition:** Blends people into teams when needed. Creates strong morale and spirit. Shares wins and successes. Fosters open dialogue. Lets people finish and be responsible for their work. Defines success in terms of the whole team. Creates a feeling of belonging in the team.

**Unskilled:** Groups they lead are not cohesive. Doesn't build shared identity. May play favourites. Doesn't manage team dynamics. Hoards information.

**Skilled:** Creates shared purpose. Manages team dynamics actively. Celebrates collective wins. Resolves internal conflicts. Builds psychological safety.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Define a team charter with your group: mission, norms, success criteria. Hold a retrospective on team dynamics (not just deliverables).
- *Education:* Read *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team* (Lencioni).

---

## 62. Time Management

**Definition:** Uses time effectively and efficiently. Values time. Concentrates efforts on the more important priorities. Gets more done in less time than others. Can attend to a broader range of activities.

**Unskilled:** Overcommitted. Disorganised. Late to meetings. Misses deadlines. Spends time on low-value activities. Can't distinguish urgent from important.

**Skilled:** Plans ahead. Batches similar work. Protects time for deep work. Says no to low-priority requests. Reliably meets deadlines.

**Development Tips:**
- *Experience:* Track your time for one week. Categorise by high/medium/low value. Eliminate or delegate the bottom 20%.
- *Education:* Read *Deep Work* (Newport) on protecting focused time.
